A Bit of Info About Limerence

This is a brief information page about limerence, which has been getting more attention in recent years.

What is limerence, exactly?...

Limerence is defined as a state of intense romantic obsession. The best way I'd personally describe it is a "crush on steroids," though it is nothing like a crush.

The term "limerence" was coined by Dorothy Tennov, who went into detail about the subject in her 1979 book, Love and Limerence. In her book, she describes this emotional state as having certain characteristics, including:

  • intrusive thinking about the object of your passionate desire (the limerent object or "LO"), who is a possible sexual partner;
  • acute longing for reciprocation;
  • dependency of mood on LO's actions or, more accurately, your interpretation of LO's actions with respect to the probability of reciprocation;
  • inability to react limerently to more than one person at a time (exceptions occur only when limerence is at low ebbâ€”early on or in the last fading);
  • fear of rejection and sometimes incapacitating but always unsettling shyness in LO's presence, especially in the beginning and whenever uncertainty strikes;
  • acute sensitivity to any act or thought or condition that can be interpreted favorably, and an extraordinary ability to devise or invent "reasonable" explanations for why the neutrality that the disinterested observer might see is in fact a sign of hidden passion in the LO;
  • buoyancy (a feeling of walking on air) when reciprocation seems evident; and
  • a general intensity of feeling that leaves other concerns in the background.

What are some misconceptions about limerence?...

On her blog Wild By Heart, life coach Stephanie Lazzara lists some common misconceptions about limerence, including:

  • Limerence is "just a crush." As stated before, this is not the case. Unlike a normal crush, limerence involves intense, intrusive thinking and interferes with other aspects of one's life to the point of disruption.
  • Limerence is something you can "get over." This idea can cause shame in those who experience it, as they are unable to easily recover from the intense emotions that come from it.
  • Limerence is all about attraction. WHile attraction is a factor, much of limerence stems from a need for emotional connection with and attention from someone, which itself stems from other factors.

Why is this important?...

Most of us have fallen in love at least once in our lives, or at least have felt something like it. Learning about limerence, and other states of mind like it, brings us one step closer to understanding ourselves and how our thoughts and emotions affect our daily lives.
